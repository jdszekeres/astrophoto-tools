Bortle Scale Reference

  1. Class 1: Excellent Dark-Sky Site
    This sky level is categorized by visible Zodiacal Light, airglow, and many faint constellations.
  2. Class 2: Typical Truly Dark Site
    This sky level is characterized by a dark sky with a well-defined Milky Way and numerous faint stars visible.
  3. Class 3: Good Dark-Sky Site
    This sky level offers a good balance between light pollution and visibility of celestial objects.
  4. Class 4: Rural Sky
    This sky level is typical of rural areas, with moderate light pollution and good visibility of the Milky Way.
  5. Class 5: Suburban Sky
    This sky level is found in suburban areas, with noticeable light pollution and limited visibility of faint objects.
  6. Class 6: Urban Sky
    This sky level is typical of urban environments, with significant light pollution and poor visibility of celestial objects.
  7. Class 7: Suburban Transition
    This sky level represents a transition zone between suburban and urban environments.
  8. Class 8: Inner City Sky
    This sky level is found in the heart of cities, with severe light pollution and minimal visibility of celestial objects.
  9. Class 9: Very Bright Urban Sky
    This sky level is characterized by extremely bright lighting conditions, making it nearly impossible to observe most celestial objects.
